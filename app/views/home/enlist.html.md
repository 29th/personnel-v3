<%= content_for :page_title, 'Enlist' %> Welcome to the 29th Infantry Division.

We are a realism unit, based on the US military system of ranks, command, and
strategy.

You are here because you wish to enlist in the 29th Infantry Division. We are
looking for dedicated, mature soldiers willing to put time and effort into
bettering their skills and strategy for the unit.

Those wishing to enlist with the 29th Infantry Division must first understand
the following:

### We do not care about your in-game skill
Our Basic Training program will teach you all the skills you need to know to
get by in a realism scrimmage. After Basic Training, our AIT program will teach
you enough about your weapon to make you much better at the game. Some of the
worst players in Darkest Hour have turned into some of the best riflemen after
two or three weeks in the 29th.

### We Communicate in English
While we understand that not everyone’s first language is English, it is our standard 
way of communicating with one another. Everything we do revolves around good communication 
and teamwork. As such, we require that new members are able to understand and be understood 
in English. That is not to say we do not accept members with English as a second language. 
But for the sake of your enjoyment and the cohesion of the team you play on, we require 
that if someone were to make a callout or give an order during a round in progress, all members 
should be able to understand and quickly communicate in turn so the entire team is on the same page. 
Whether that be verbally or over text chat.

### 29th Infantry Division Professionalism
While this is just a game, we provide an experience that promotes professionalism, community, and competency. 
If in Basic Training you are wondering why someone is yelling at you, don’t take it personally - it’s their job to prepare you for the 29th. 
Just turn on Full Metal Jacket and be thankful that <%= link_to 'Gunnery Sergeant Hartman', 'https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tHxf17yJsKs' %> isn’t your Drill Instructor. 
In the long run, our adherence to professionalism and discipline leads to a fun and enjoyable team environment.

### Leave your Politics at the Door
We are here to participate in a realism unit, to function as a well-oiled machine of teamwork and skill. 
Frankly, we don’t care about your gender, race, religion, political stance, social, or economic standing. 
Your success or failure with the unit is purely due to your performance and behavior during our drills or 
in our TS, Discord, and Forums. We strive to keep this large group of people working together easily 
and effectively. As such, we have a long tradition of being a neutral ground when it comes to politics 
and world events. So, when you join the 29th, leave your politics at the door; it’s not just encouraged, it’s required.

### Our schedule is accommodating
Some get overwhelmed when they hear they need to attend Basic Combat Training to join the 29th. 
However, our Training Staff offer a range of dates and times to best match your schedule and get you through your one day of class. 
You will then have one day of seeing a real 29th Drill. More importantly, after Basic, your schedule is 
roughly two, one hour sessions per week – and you can miss those if something comes up! 
There are so many different drill times that we can always work something out to fit your schedule.

### We focus on one weapon at a time
All of our soldiers are trained with their specialty weapon. As we are an Infantry Battalion, most of our members are 
Riflemen - and very good ones. Each recruit starts out using only rifles to develop a solid rifleman foundation. 
After showing competency with the Rifleman kit, you may apply and be selected for Advanced Training in a specialty weapon system.
